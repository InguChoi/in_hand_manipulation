Lessons from Learning Object Rotation for Sim-to-Real Deployment of In-Hand Manipulation Policies

IEEE ICRA 2026 Workshop Dex Submission
Ingu Choi1,2, Magnus Hanes1, Sungwoo Park3
1Fraunhofer IFF, 2Telescope Innovations Corp., 3KIST
This work was conducted at Fraunhofer IFF.
Keywords: Dexterous In-Hand Manipulation, Re-orientation, Sim-to-Real
Paper Video

Video

Abstract

Recent advances in reinforcement learning have enabled significant progress in dexterous in-hand manipulation. However, sim-to-real transfer remains a major challenge, often due to discrepancies between simulated and real-world observations. In this work, we revisit a fundamental question: does providing more observations improve policy performance? To answer this, we conduct a systematic ablation study on observation design for in-hand object reorientation using a real robotic platform. We evaluate multiple policy variants with different combinations of object state, fingertip pose, velocity, torque, and contact signals.

Our results reveal two key findings. First, observations that suffer from distribution mismatch, such as velocity, torque, and explicitly modeled contact signals, consistently degrade real-world performance. These signals introduce noise and inconsistency between simulation and reality, leading to unstable and oscillatory behavior. Second, geometrically consistent observations, particularly fingertip pose derived from forward kinematics, significantly improve stability and control performance. Overall, we show that increasing observation richness does not necessarily improve policy performance. Instead, carefully designed observations that are consistent across domains are essential for robust sim-to-real transfer. These findings provide practical guidelines for designing deployable policies in contact-rich robotic manipulation tasks.

Method Overview

We study in-hand object reorientation where a robotic hand must rotate a grasped object to match a target orientation. The policy is trained with PPO in Isaac Sim and directly deployed on real hardware through ROS 2, without architectural modification between simulation and deployment.

Results

We summarize the ablation study with both a radar-chart comparison and a table of metrics to highlight how each observation design affects sim-to-real deployment performance.
